Knowledge Work (P. Drucker)

  1. Knowledge-worker productivity demands that we ask the question: ‚ÄúWhat is the task?‚Äù
  2. It demands that we impose the responsibility for their productivity on the individual knowledge workers themselves. Knowledge workers have to manage themselves. They have to have autonomy.
  3. Continuing innovation has to be part of the work, the task and the responsibility of knowledge workers.
  4. Knowledge work requires continuous learning on the part of the knowledge worker, but equally continuous teaching on the part of the knowledge worker.
  5. Productivity of the knowledge worker is not‚Äîat least not primarily‚Äîa matter of the quantity of output. Quality is at least as important.
  6. Finally, knowledge-worker productivity requires that the knowledge worker is both seen and treated as an ‚Äúasset‚Äù rather than a ‚Äúcost.‚Äù It requires that knowledge workers want to work for the organization in preference to all other opportunities.
  • Management expert Peter Drucker highlighted a few major factors that determine knowledge worker productivity
  • Autonomy (1)
  • Innovation (3)
  • Continuous learning (4)

Where Should I Start?

The HBR artikel Where Should Your Company Start with GenAI? provides a framwork to analyse tasks that will be highly affected by GenAI.
  • Paul Baier and colleaques (GAI Insights) identified a subclass of knowledge tasks that are highly affected by GenAI
  • WINS work: words, images, numbers, sounds (e.g. programmers, accountants, marketing professionals)
  • Crucible: understand and embrace GenAI immediately (so competitors do not adopt faster); Balcony (landscaping, house painting, spa service
